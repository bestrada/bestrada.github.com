Susan

Hi, I’m Susan and this is Prashant, Theresa, Siva and Bryan.
The ceremony was so sweet, it’s amazing we aren’t all still cryin’.
So, here we are the group that Andrew calls the fam,
and we’re all here to tell you why Andrew is the man.

For 3 years, we used to work together at Microsoft in Mountain View,
though the number of days that we weren’t together were just a few.
Every day, we carpooled to work and went out to lunch,
We even had a sleepover because we didn’t see enough of this bunch.
Group halloween costumes, secret santas, elaborate birthdays were how we liked to roll,
but I have to say, that of the group, Andrew was the heart and soul.

So some might say that Andrew is an intense fella,
but to us, we lovingly know that Andrew is just a bit “hella.”
He even dubbed the two us, when we hung out, Team Huwang.
On one of our hangouts, I noticed he was singing a new sweet song.

He told me about this great girl, and I thought wow, could this be true?
Could this girl be just as hella as Andrew?
Their first date turned into a 5 stop walking food tour,
so that’s how I knew how he could be so sure.

An 8-hour date meant Andrew finally found his match,
I knew this intense, hella foodie girl was definitely a catch!

Prashant

Even now, Andrew brings the same intensity to our friendship.
One day, while we were biking through the park at quite a clip,
I showed him a hidden bike track, a diamond in the rough.
Perfect for Andrew, because an easy ride is not nearly enough.

His excitement overtook him, and he raced around the cement.
“Maybe this was a bad idea,” I started to lament.
He suggested we do a couple of laps, which somehow turned into nine.
The next time we rode, we pushed for a dozen, which on their own seemed fine.
But Of course we finished the rest of the ride, because exhaustion would never stop Andrew
Then without missing a beat, he turned a post-ride bite into a fun meal with the crew.

And that meal had fried fish, rice pudding, beignets, and two bahn mis
Basically we scoured the city for whatever we could shove in our tummies
As Andrew headed home for his next meal, and I was about to collapse,
he told me don't worry, next time we'll do even more laps.

Let me tell you a story of Andrew’s intense love and passion for.. food
It takes place in New Zealand, where tender lamb and oysters set the mood
Our first night out, Andrew found these diamond clams that were so amazing, we had a second plate.
We devoured them all, except for the two lonely closed ones that wondered if anyone would take the bait.

But Andrew will leave no man, or clam, behind!
A more loyal (or hungry) fellow, you’ll never find.
He took a butter knife, cracked them open, and won that battle
The next day, however, the revenge of clams made sure his stomach felt a rattle.
You see, closed clams are typically not safe to eat.
But don’t worry, Andrew and his love of food will never be beat.

Andrew, Mary, and I grew closer throughout this New Zealand trip
Helicopter rides and black water rafting really cemented our friendship.
On the last night, Andrew and Mary were so sweet to invite me to their dinner date
I realized quickly that I was welcome not only as a friend, but as a third shared plate!

Siva

I’m Andrew’s old roommate and so I know him quite well.
The passion and intensity he carried wrapped you up as those who know him can tell.

My first meeting with this fellow was on route to a party with many a friend
I couldn’t help humming the Disney song A Whole New World; I like Disney, I can’t pretend.
Now for this hummed show most folk would stare and the awkwardness spikes
But Andrew and I belted; shoulder to shoulder swaying as we traded verses in our air mics.

Now I knew this man was someone to keep close, so I joined him as his roommate
I like a good adventure and had plenty from Andrew’s intensity that does not abate.
Though his interests were varied there was one that maintained: food
One memorable instance, was our ramen making which I joined not to be rude.
The morning after the first time, I could see the joy on his face and knew we’d make quite a few
It must have been ten or fifteen times until we hit his recipe: double meat, lots of egg, extra veggies, fully loaded as only Andrew can do.

A great tradition we had as roommates was watching comedy nights, thursdays, on tv
Andrew was unsurprisingly “hella” but frankly looking forward to those nights gave me glee
Around this time I met Mary and she gave me a nickname that she still fondly uses: Nemesis
Why you ask? For her she felt competition for Andrew’s attention from me and she was going to win this.

What she didn’t understand was that I wasn’t battling because there was a clear victor to this thing
It was obvious that Mary was the person Andrew would devote his passion and intensity to right from the beginning

Bryan

One evening, Amy and Mary offered to cook dinner at their place off Polk street.
Me, Anqi, and Landon agreed to come over to share a bite to eat.
Before we arrived, Mary asked to us to pick up a few things at the store.
Anqi and I drove out to the Sunset to retrieve what she asked for.

After we left the market, Anqi remembered a missing ingredient much to his dismay.
He said “let’s just go back to my house, it’s totally on the way”.
If you know Anqi, you know his internal compass is a little bit obscure.
His house was definitely out of the way; a complete detour.
I realized what he was thinking, let me explain: no joke,
Anqi has a special navigation scheme -- I call it “hub and spoke”.

In his mind, he can drive back and forth from anywhere as long as he starts from his home.
His apartment is on the way to everywhere because he touches base before he starts to roam.
I’m not confident Anqi will ever graduate from the “I’m always lost” club,
But I do know for Mary – she’s no longer a spoke, she’s his hub.
